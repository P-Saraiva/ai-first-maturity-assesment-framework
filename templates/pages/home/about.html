Purpose of the Assessment

This assessment helps organizations understand, measure, and manage their maturity in the use of Artificial Intelligence from a trust, security, risk, and governance perspective.

Rather than focusing solely on technical performance, the assessment evaluates AI systems as socio-technical systems, where risks emerge from the interaction between:

  • Technology
  • People
  • Processes
  • Data
  • Governance structures

The primary goal is to enable organizations to identify material AI risks early, understand root causes, and prioritize practical improvements aligned with frameworks such as OWASP AIMA, NIST AI RMF, and MITRE ATLAS.

The Mission

This project has the goal to democratize AI-first and secure software engineering by providing organizations with comprehensive assessment tools, actionable insights, and rich resources that accelerate their digital transformation and competitive advantage.

The Vision

The vision is to move organizations from reactive, compliance-driven AI practices toward proactive, risk-informed, and maturity-driven AI governance.

  • AI risks are anticipated rather than discovered after incidents.
  • Ethical, legal, and security considerations are embedded into business and operational processes.
  • Decision-making is supported by evidence, metrics, and continuous monitoring.
  • AI systems remain trustworthy, secure, and aligned with organizational values throughout their lifecycle.
This assessment is:
  • A maturity-based diagnostic tool, not a pass/fail audit
  • A risk-oriented framework, not a checklist
  • A way to surface hidden or emerging AI risks (incl. governance)
  • A foundation for prioritized, roadmap-driven improvement
This assessment is not:
  • A regulatory certification
  • A one-time compliance exercise
  • A purely technical evaluation of model performance
  • A substitute for technical audits or penetration testing

Maturity Levels

The maturity model reflects how consistently and effectively AI-related practices are implemented across the organization. Each area is evaluated across five maturity levels:

Level 1
Informal

Ad-hoc level. Practices are reactive and inconsistent; risks often addressed after incidents.

Level 2
Defined

Basic controls exist; practices depend heavily on individuals.

Level 3
Systematic

Systematic AI integration across development workflows. Processes are documented, standardized, and repeatable; risk, ethics, and security are embedded.

Level 4
Integrated

Practices are measured; decisions informed by data, monitoring, and trends.

Level 5
Optimized

Continuous improvement; lessons and emerging risks are systematically incorporated.
